Piano

Watch Demo Video

Developed a modular electronic keyboard system based on STM32 and FreeRTOS, where each keyboard module contained seven keys and up to four modules could be cascaded to form a complete instrument. The modules communicated and coordinated via a CAN bus, enabling scalable system integration. The system supported configurable parameters such as pitch, timbre, and sustain, demonstrating embedded system design capabilities in real-time multitasking and inter-module communication.

Mini Tank

View on GitHub

This project is a remote-controlled mini tank built using a Raspberry Pi 5. It features four Mecanum wheels for omnidirectional movement (including sideways motion) and a gun turret mounted on a 2-axis gimbal that can adjust its angle for precise aiming. The tank is operated through a web-based control interface hosted on the Raspberry Pi, allowing users on the same Wi-Fi network to drive and control the turret from a browser. The design emphasizes real-time responsiveness and intuitive control, making it a fun robotics platform for experimentation.

VEX Robotics Competition

The VEX Robotics Competition was my favorite project throughout high school and the deepest source of motivation behind my decision to pursue engineering. Starting with nothing more than a pile of individual parts, I experienced the entire engineering process—from mechanical design and assembly to programming and debugging—until the robot could successfully complete complex tasks on the field. Seeing our ideas take physical form and perform as intended gave me an immense sense of accomplishment. Through countless iterations, failures, and improvements, I discovered the creativity, logic, and impact embedded in engineering, and this experience ultimately led me to develop a genuine passion for the field.
